They Knew. And They Didn’t Care.

Names, dates, and the documented relationships of current U.S. officeholders to a convicted sex trafficker.

Use this when Use this when someone asks what the files actually show about the people currently in power, or when the conversation needs grounding in documented facts rather than allegation.

I want to be specific. I am going to name names. Because one of the extraordinary things about the Epstein files is that the network of people who knew, participated, or simply did not ask questions is no longer abstract. It is documented. And several of those people currently hold the highest offices in the United States government.

This is not speculation. This is the documented record.

Howard Lutnick — Commerce Secretary

Lutnick has maintained he cut off all contact with Epstein after a 2005 visit to his Upper East Side townhouse, telling a podcast in October 2025 that Epstein was “disgustin” and that he was “never in the room with him socially, for business or even philanthropy” after that date. He appears two hundred twenty-three times in the files.

The files shattered his claims across a thirteen-year documented timeline. A May 2011 calendar entry shows Epstein scheduling drinks with Lutnick at five p.m. In December 2012, emails show Lutnick and his wife coordinating a visit to Epstein’s private island, Little St. James. He asked for coordinates for his boat captain and proposed dinner, arriving on a one hundred eighty-eight-foot yacht with his wife, another couple, and both families’ four children—ages seven to sixteen.

On December 28, 2012, both Lutnick and Epstein signed contracts to acquire stakes in AdFin Solutions Inc., a digital advertising company—a business partnership that continued through at least 2014. In 2015, Lutnick tried to invite Epstein to a Hillary Clinton fundraiser. In 2017, Epstein agreed to donate fifty thousand dollars to a dinner honoring Lutnick. Their last documented communication was May 2018.

Representative Thomas Massie, co-author of the Epstein Files Transparency Act, called on Lutnick to resign on CNN on February 8, 2026. At a Senate hearing two days later, Lutnick confirmed visiting the island but insisted: “I did not have any relationship with him” and “I have done absolutely nothing wrong in any possible regard.”

He is now Commerce Secretary of the United States.

The Broader Network

The files document correspondence between Epstein and members of the global elite that continued after his 2008 conviction—after he was a publicly registered sex offender.

Prince Andrew continued correspondence through at least February 2011. He settled Virginia Giuffre’s civil lawsuit in 2022 for an estimated twelve million pounds. He remains in Royal Lodge on the Windsor estate.

Bill Gates maintained relationships post-2008. The files document multiple meetings and email exchanges. Gates acknowledged the meetings publicly only after media exposure.

Steve Tisch sent multiple emails seeking Epstein’s help procuring women for sex—years after the conviction. He still co-owns the New York Giants.

Kevin Warsh, Trump’s nominee for Federal Reserve Chair, appeared on a 2010 Christmas gathering invite list. If confirmed, he will set interest rates that affect every American’s mortgage, car loan, and credit card.

Donald Trump appears thousands of times in the files. Photographs of Trump were found in Epstein’s home. The files do not contain criminal allegations against Trump, but the volume of references documents the depth and duration of association with a man who was running a child sex trafficking operation.

These men either directly participated in the exploitation of children, or they maintained social and professional relationships with a known, convicted sex offender of children. After 2008, this was not hidden. Epstein’s status as a sex offender was public knowledge.

They knew. They did not care.

The Accountability Paradox

The current administration signed the Epstein Files Transparency Act. It passed with bipartisan support. It was framed as a commitment to accountability and public disclosure.

The same administration appointed as DOJ leadership people who have interests in ensuring accountability does not reach certain members of that network. The same administration has nominated to senior positions individuals named in the documents.

The release of the files has produced extraordinary public interest. It has not, as of February 2026, produced a single new charge. The DOJ has stated it does not intend to file new charges. The people most likely to be charged are the people whose colleagues and associates now run the institution with charging authority.

Meanwhile, European governments have moved faster. France arrested and charged Epstein associates. The contrast is instructive: the system works when the powerful people implicated do not also control the system of accountability.

I do not know what accountability looks like from here. I do not know if it comes from Congress, from state prosecutors, from civil litigation, from continued investigative journalism. I know it is not coming from the federal apparatus currently in place.

I know that eighty victims have been identified. Zero co-conspirators have been charged. And the men whose names appear in the files are currently running the country.

They knew. They did not care. And they are still in charge.
